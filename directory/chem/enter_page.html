Hess's law: cancel out everything except products and reactants. Hess's law: overall reaction enthalpy is the sum of reaction enthalpies of steps into which the reaction can be divided.
Standard enthalpies of formation: formation of substance in their most stable form
note: the standard enthalpy of formation of an Element in its most stable form: 0

Standard Enthalpy of formation of C(gr), graphite, is 0. Graphite --> graphite is a null reaction. Nothing changes. Most stable form: already formed.

However, the conversion of carbon to graphite is Nonzero. C(gr) --> c(diamond) deltaH = +1.9 kJ Diamond is not carbon graphite's stable form.

First, we convert reactants-->elements in stable form. C(diamond)-->c(graphite).

And then, elements--> products

At first, we need to calculate. Formation of products. Then calculate formation of reactants from elements.

The difference: moles enthalpy formation Products - moles enthalpy formation reactants = enthalpy formation of Reaction
